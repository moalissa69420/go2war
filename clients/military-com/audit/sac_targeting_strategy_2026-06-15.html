Targeting Strategy — SAC-Verified (Jun 15, 2026)

Supersedes the Jun 11–13 audience architecture (the lookalike ladders). Researched and verified across multiple 2025–26 sources + tested live in the account.

The verified reality: under Financial SAC, audience-precision targeting is gone

The account runs under Meta's Financial Products & Services Special Ad Category. Verified — every audience-precision lever is unavailable:

LeverStatus under Financial SAC
Standard lookalikes❌ Blocked (confirmed live — won't appear in ad set picker)
Special Ad Audiences (the old lookalike replacement)❌ Discontinued entirely by Meta
Advantage+ Audience expansion❌ Not permitted for Financial SAC — Original audiences only
Detailed targeting / interests❌ Blocked
Age / gender / ZIP narrowing❌ Locked to 18–65, US, 15-mi min radius
Custom audiences (the seeds)⚠️ Allowed, but in Original mode they constrain (no expansion → small). Only genuinely useful as exclusions.

Conclusion: there is no audience-targeting precision under SAC. You optimize and create your way to performance — you do not target your way there.

The audience structure (every prospecting ad set — identical)

  • Mode: Original audiences (Advantage+ unavailable under SAC)
  • Targeting: Broad — 18–65, US (locked)
  • Includes: none (lookalikes blocked; seed-includes constrain to ~seed size; Special Ad Audiences gone)
  • Exclude: GTW_Mil_AllCustomers_7mo_06-13 (suppression — existing customers)
  • Account segments (already set): Existing customers + Engaged

That's the whole audience setup. It does not differ by campaign — because under SAC it can't.

What actually wins under SAC (ranked — this is the real strategy)

Research is emphatic: "success depends heavily on creative quality and conversion optimization rather than granular audience targeting."

  • Creative — the #1 lever. Under broad targeting, creative IS your targeting (it self-selects who responds). Restricted-category data shows 40%+ CTR swings from creative testing. Ship volume + iterate.
  • Conversion optimization / the value loop. Optimizing for the right event (real accepted leads via the QW data loop) drives up to 31% lower CPL. This is how you get quality when you can't target for it.
  • Suppression. Exclude existing customers → efficiency. (The one piece of the audience build that fully deploys.)
  • Cost caps + budget. The spend lever (loosen caps to deploy idle budget).

Note: SAC carries a ~23% higher CPM (broad-targeting tax) — unavoidable, baked into the model.

What to STOP doing

  • ❌ Don't deploy the 12 lookalike ladders — blocked under SAC
  • ❌ Don't use seeds as prospecting includes — they constrain to ~seed size
  • ❌ Don't build Special Ad Audiences — discontinued

What the audience work IS still worth

  • ✅ Suppression list (exclusion) — real efficiency lever, deployed
  • ✅ Existing-customers + Engaged segments — set, working
  • ✅ The accepted-lead / seed DATA — feeds the value loop (the actual targeting mechanism under SAC)
  • ✅ Fresh, clean, compliant first-party data — the foundation the value loop runs on

The roadmap — "things we can do" under SAC (in priority order)

  • Creative engine — the highest-leverage lever bar none under broad. Ship a steady volume of test creatives, kill losers fast, scale winners. This is where the account is actually won.
  • Value loop — get the QW accepted-lead export → push to Meta (offline conversions / CRM upload) → optimize Max Val for real accepted-lead value. This is how you beat plain broad. Replaces what lookalikes were meant to do.
  • Suppression — applied as exclusion on every prospecting ad set (deployed).
  • Cost-cap loosening — Auto BAU $15→$23, Home $10→$16, stepped up with guardrails (CPL < ~$26 Auto / $18 Home; Delty value/lead holds ~$32).
  • Concentrate budget in the winners (BAU campaigns) — let the redundant ALC campaigns self-deprioritize.

The honest strategic reframe

The audience month surfaced the suppression lever (real) and the clean first-party data (foundation), but more importantly it proved that under SAC the account is won on creative + the value/conversion loop — not audiences. Both were already in scope; the research just promoted them from "also important" to the whole game. That's a more valuable conclusion than another lookalike ladder.
