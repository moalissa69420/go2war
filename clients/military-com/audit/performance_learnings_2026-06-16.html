What we learned

1. Gradual cost-cap steps unlock volume without breaking efficiency

  • We stepped the cost-per-result goals up +$2 per campaign (Auto BAU $17 → ~$19), with more steps to go.
  • That single step drove spend from ~$20K/day → $31–35K/day — the record days.
  • Value/lead HELD at ~$37 through the increase. The feared efficiency erosion did not happen. Gradual stepping (vs a big jump) kept the ad sets out of re-learning.

2. CPL cools through the day — don't panic on a hot morning

  • Auto BAU CPL on Jun 16: $26.97 (morning) → $24.70 → $23.62 → $21.84 (afternoon).
  • Meta over-delivers cost early while it re-optimizes, then settles down. A hot 10am CPL is not the day's number. Read CPL late, not early.

3. Daily performance is driven by VALUE/LEAD, not just CPL

  • Across June, weak days (Jun 6/7/9) all had ~$26 value/lead; strong days (Jun 10/11/13/15) had $36–38.
  • The ~45% swing in value/lead is the swing between losing and record days. This is why the value loop is the #1 structural lever — pulling the floor up from $26 toward $37 eliminates the losing days.

4. The intraday Delty/Meta clock mismatch flatters ROAS

  • Meta runs Pacific; Delty runs ~3 hours ahead. Intraday, Delty revenue is "ahead" of Meta spend, so live ROAS reads high (~1.9x) and settles lower by EOD. Judge the day on the per-lead ratio and the final close, not the intraday ROAS.
